# Cold Email - SecureStack to James Liu

**Framework Used:** Trigger -> Insight -> Ask

---

## Metadata

- **To:** James Liu, VP of Engineering
- **Company:** FinScale
- **Company Size:** 300 employees, ~30 engineers
- **Stage:** Series B
- **Trigger Signal:** Recently SOC 2 compliant
- **Goal:** Reply expressing interest
- **Product:** SecureStack (developer security training platform)

---

## Subject Line

`soc 2 follow-through`

---

## Email Body

Congrats on the SOC 2 milestone -- that's a real lift for a fintech at your stage.

The pattern I've seen is that getting compliant is the hard part. Keeping it? That's where most teams slip. Especially when you're scaling from 30 to 50 engineers and half of them haven't internalized the security culture yet.

We worked with PaySecure (250-person fintech) after their SOC 2. Within 6 months they'd cut security incidents by 40% -- not because they added more process, but because their engineers started catching issues before they became incidents.

Curious if you've thought about how to scale the security culture alongside your headcount. Worth a quick conversation?

## Why This Works

- **Triggered:** Opens on their SOC 2 win, not your product. Shows you know their situation.
- **Peer voice:** Reads like a note from someone who's been through this, not a pitch.
- **Specific problem:** Scales security culture during headcount growth -- a concrete, relatable challenge for a VP of Engineering.
- **Proof > features:** One result (40% incident reduction) beats listing 10 platform features.
- **Low-friction CTA:** "Worth a quick conversation?" is easy to reply yes to. No calendar link, no 30-minute booking pressure.
- **No red flags:** No "I hope this email finds you well," no jargon, no HTML, no multiple links.
- **Subject line:** Looks internal, boring, not salesy. Gets opened.

## What I'd A/B Test

- **Subject line variant:** `scaling eng security`
- **Opening variant:** Lead with a question instead of the congratulatory note: "How do you keep 50 engineers sharp on security when 20 of them are new?"
- **CTA variant:** "Open to swapping notes on how other fintechs handle this?"
