# Annotations

## Headline Choice
**Why "Your clients get a weekly report. You don't have to build it.":**
- Opens with the outcome clients want (reports delivered)
- Immediately addresses the pain (building them)
- Uses second person to speak directly to the reader
- Principle: Lead with the benefit, not the feature

## Problem Section
**Why "The report-building trap is eating your margins":**
- Frames the problem as a business cost, not an inconvenience
- "Margins" is agency language — speaks to profitability
- Specific time frames (2-4 hours, Friday afternoon) create recognition
- Principle: Use customer language over company language

## Social Proof Structure
**Why specific metrics + named testimonials:**
- Named, titled testimonials build credibility over anonymous quotes
- "200+ agencies" provides scale proof
- "3.2 hours per week per analyst" is specific enough to be believable
- Principle: Specificity over vagueness

## CTA Copy
**Why "Start Free Trial" over alternatives:**
- Direct and action-oriented
- "Free" reduces perceived risk
- "Trial" implies low commitment
- No jargon, no ambiguity
- Principle: Clear over clever

## Objection Handling
**Why address the "AI gets things wrong" concern explicitly:**
- Marketing directors worry about client-facing errors
- Positioning the human-in-the-loop review addresses trust
- Reframes AI as draft-writer, not final-author
- Principle: Show over tell
