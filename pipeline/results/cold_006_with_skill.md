# Cold Email - RouteOptima to David Park

**Framework Used:** Observation -> Problem -> Proof -> Ask

---

## Metadata

- **To:** David Park, CEO
- **Company:** GreenLeaf Logistics
- **Company Size:** 400 employees
- **Industry:** Logistics (traditional, likely less tech-savvy)
- **Goal:** Reply expressing interest
- **Product:** RouteOptima (AI route optimization tool)

---

## Subject Line

`fuel spend`

---

## Email Body

David,

At 400 people, your dispatch team is probably still building routes manually or running legacy software that hasn't kept up. The result: fuel costs that creep up and delivery windows that slip.

We helped FastFreight -- similar size, similar space -- cut fuel costs 18% and push on-time delivery from 87% to 96%. That's real money back to the bottom line, not a dashboard that looks nice.

Worth a 10-minute look?

---

## Copy Breakdown

| Section | Purpose | Line Used |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Observation** | Anchor to their likely current state | "Your dispatch team is probably still building routes manually or running legacy software that hasn't kept up." |
| **Problem** | Name the pain they're living with | "Fuel costs that creep up and delivery windows that slip." |
| **Proof** | Concrete result from a comparable company | "FastFreight cut fuel costs 18% and pushed on-time delivery from 87% to 96%." |
| **Ask** | Ultra-low-friction CTA for C-suite | "Worth a 10-minute look?" |

## Why This Works

- **Peer voice:** Opens on their situation, not your product. Reads like a note from someone who knows logistics, not a vendor.
- **C-suite calibration:** No jargon, no pitch, no calendar link. Ultra-brief. David can scan this in 8 seconds.
- **Observation lands:** "400 people" shows you know their scale. "Manual routes or legacy software" names the likely reality without assuming too much.
- **Proof is the payload:** One result (18% fuel savings, 87% -> 96% on-time) does all the heavy lifting. No feature dump.
- **Low-friction CTA:** "Worth a 10-minute look?" is easy to reply yes to. No meeting request, no demo pressure, no link to click.
- **No red flags:** No "I hope this email finds you well," no jargon, no HTML, no multiple links, no fake urgency.
- **Subject line:** Looks like an internal metric, not a sales email. Gets opened.

## What I'd A/B Test

- **Subject line variant:** `route costs`
- **Opening variant:** Lead with the proof first: "FastFreight saved 18% on fuel. Curious if the math works for GreenLeaf too."
- **CTA variant:** "Open to a quick comparison of what this looks like at your scale?"
