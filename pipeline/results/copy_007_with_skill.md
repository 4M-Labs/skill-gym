# FreshBooks Cloud — 7-Page Website CTA Copy

## Brief

**Product:** FreshBooks Cloud — cloud accounting software with invoicing, expense tracking, time tracking, and tax prep.

**Audience:** Freelancers and solo consultants who hate bookkeeping.

**Voice:** Friendly, reassuring, slightly playful.

**Primary action across all pages:** Start a free trial.

---

# 1. Homepage — Hero CTA

## Page Copy

**Headline:**
Your clients pay you to do what you love. The bookkeeping part? That's where we come in.

**Subheadline:**
Invoicing, expenses, time tracking, and tax prep — built for freelancers who'd rather work than chase receipts.

**Primary CTA:**
Start Free for 30 Days

**Secondary CTA:**
See How It Works

---

## Annotations

**Headline — "Your clients pay you to do what you love. The bookkeeping part? That's where we come in."**
Rhetorical structure splits the reader's identity (freelancer doing real work) from the pain (bookkeeping). "That's where we come in" is a warm handoff, not a hard sell. The period after "part" creates a beat — it mirrors the pause a freelancer takes when they remember they have to reconcile their expenses.

**Subheadline — "Invoicing, expenses, time tracking, and tax prep — built for freelancers who'd rather work than chase receipts."**
Lists the four core features without sounding like a spec sheet. "Chase receipts" is specific and relatable — every freelancer has done this. The dash creates a natural pivot from what we do to who we do it for.

**CTA — "Start Free for 30 Days"**
"Free" reduces risk. "30 Days" sets a specific expectation. "Start" is action-oriented and implies momentum. No "sign up" or "get started" — those are generic and passive.

**Secondary CTA — "See How It Works"**
Lower commitment path for visitors who aren't ready to commit. "See" is visual and concrete — implies a demo or walkthrough, not a sales call.

---

## Alternatives

### Headline Options

**Option A:** Your clients pay you to do what you love. The bookkeeping part? That's where we come in. — Identity-first framing. Works because it validates the freelancer's real work before addressing the pain.

**Option B:** Freelancing is great. Bookkeeping is not. Let's fix that. — Shorter, punchier. "Let's fix that" is collaborative and direct. Works for audiences who prefer no-nonsense copy.

**Option C:** You started your own business to call the shots — not to spend Sundays doing invoices. — Specific scenario that every freelancer has lived. "Sundays" makes it visceral and personal.

### CTA Options

**Option A:** Start Free for 30 Days — Risk-free trial with clear time boundary.

**Option B:** Try FreshBooks Free — Simpler. "Try" is lower commitment than "Start."

**Option C:** Get Your Free Trial Now — "Your" adds ownership. "Now" creates urgency without pressure.

## Annotations

**Headline — "Simple pricing. No surprises. No hidden fees."**
Three short sentences that address the #1 pricing page anxiety: hidden costs. "No surprises" is reassuring. "No hidden fees" is the specific version of that promise. This headline earns trust before the visitor even looks at numbers.

**Subheadline — "Every plan includes a 30-day free trial. No credit card required to start."**
Two risk-reversal statements stacked. "30-day" is specific. "No credit card required" removes the biggest barrier to trying. Placed above the tiers so every visitor sees this before evaluating price.

**Tier names — Starter / Growth / Pro**
Three names that signal progression without judgment. "Starter" doesn't feel cheap — it feels appropriate for someone beginning. "Growth" implies ambition. "Pro" implies mastery. None of them feel like a consolation prize.

**CTAs — "Start Free with Starter / Growth / Pro"**
Each CTA names the tier. This reduces confusion — the visitor knows exactly what they're choosing. "Start Free" is repeated in every tier to reinforce that no money is needed today.

**"No credit card required to start"**
Placed at the top, not buried in fine print. This is the single most effective phrase on a pricing page for trial conversion.

---

### Headline Options

**Option A:** Simple pricing. No surprises. No hidden fees. — Trust-first approach. Addresses anxiety before presenting options.

**Option B:** Pick the plan that fits. Upgrade when you're ready. — Growth-oriented. Implies flexibility and removes commitment pressure.

## Cross-Page CTA Strategy

### Consistency
The primary CTA across all pages is **"Start Free for 30 Days"** (or slight variations). This creates a single, clear conversion goal. Visitors should never wonder what to do next.

### CTA Progression by Page

| Page | Primary CTA | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Homepage Hero | Start Free for 30 Days | Highest intent — capture immediately |
| Homepage Bottom | Start Free for 30 Days | Reinforce after scrolling through proof |
| Features | Start Free for 30 Days | Post-education conversion |
| Pricing | Start Free with [Tier] | Tier-specific reduces decision friction |
| Case Studies | Join 30 Million... | Social proof baked into CTA |
| About | Try FreshBooks Free | Exploratory — lower pressure |
| Contact | Send Us a Message | Service-oriented — different goal |

### Risk Reversal Phrases (use 2-3 times per page)
- "30-day free trial"
- "No credit card required"
- "Cancel anytime"

### Secondary CTA Patterns
- "See How It Works" (education path)
- "Talk to Our Team" (support path)
- "Read the Docs" (self-service path)

These secondary CTAs ensure no visitor hits a dead end. Every page offers at least two paths forward.
