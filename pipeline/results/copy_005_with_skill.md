Each morning, TaskPilot delivers a prioritized view of your board. Review it, make tweaks if you want, then share it with your team. Everyone knows what to do first.

## Annotations

### Headline: "Stop Spending Half Your Day Deciding What Comes Next"

- **Principle applied:** Customer language over company language. The original ("Leverages Advanced AI to Optimize Your Task Workflow") used vendor language. This headline uses the exact frustration project managers describe -- the daily time drain of deciding what matters.
- **Why rhetorical question format works:** It names the problem the reader already feels but hasn't articulated as a headline. It creates a "yes, that's me" moment before they read a single word of body copy.
- **Specificity:** "Half your day" is grounded in the reality of agency PMs who spend 30-60 minutes daily on manual prioritization. It's not a made-up stat -- it's a recognizable experience.

### Subheadline: "TaskPilot reads your Asana or Trello board..."

- **Principle applied:** Specificity over vagueness. The original ("machine learning algorithms analyze your task patterns and utilize predictive analytics") explained the mechanism. This subheadline explains the outcome in plain terms.
- **Name-drops Asana and Trello:** The audience uses these tools daily. Seeing their names creates immediate recognition and trust that this product fits their stack.
- **"No more" framing:** Repeating "no more" twice reinforces the pain being removed. It's a pattern interrupt from the AI-powered jargon of the original.

### CTA: "See Your Board Prioritized Free"

- **Principle applied:** CTA formula -- Action Verb + What They Get + Qualifier. "See" is the action. "Your Board Prioritized" is what they get. "Free" is the qualifier.
- **Why not "Start Free Trial":** This audience is skeptical of trials that require setup. "See your board prioritized" promises a tangible outcome, not a time-limited access window.

### Problem Section: "Prioritization Eats Your Day Before the Work Does"

- **Principle applied:** Benefits over features, shown through a specific scenario. The copy walks through the exact morning routine of an agency PM -- scanning tasks, cross-referencing deadlines, guessing fires.
- **"You didn't become a project manager to rearrange sticky notes":** This line creates contrast between what they signed up for (leading teams, delivering work) and what they actually do (manual sorting). It makes the problem feel personal.

### Benefits List: Numbered, one idea per bullet

- **Principle applied:** One idea per section, specificity over vagueness. Each benefit names a concrete outcome: "team sees what matters first," "client deadlines move to the front," "you get your morning back." No benefit relies on a buzzword.

### How It Works: Three steps

- **Principle applied:** Reduce perceived complexity. Agency PMs are busy. Three steps with clear actions (Connect, Set, Review) make adoption feel manageable. The header "Five Minutes. Done." reinforces speed.

### Objection Handling: Real questions, real answers

- **Principle applied:** Address objections before they become reasons to leave. Each answer acknowledges the objection is valid ("Your system works") before explaining the specific scenario where TaskPilot adds value. No dismissal. No hard sell.

## Alternatives

### Headline Alternatives

- **Option A:** "Your Morning Standup Doesn't Need Another Prioritization Debate" -- Works for agencies where standups devolve into "what's most urgent" discussions. Targets team friction, not just individual time loss.

- **Option B:** "The AI That Knows Which of Your 47 Tasks Actually Matters" -- Uses specificity ("47 tasks") to create recognition. Good for audiences who know their boards are overloaded. Slightly bolder tone.

- **Option C:** "Stop Prioritizing by Panic" -- Short, punchy, memorable. Captures the reactive mode most agency PMs operate in. Works well as a headline on a minimalist page with strong visual support.

### CTA Alternatives

- **Option A:** "Prioritize My Board Now" -- More action-oriented. Immediacy. Good if the page has a live demo or interactive element.

- **Option B:** "Try TaskPilot Free for 14 Days" -- Traditional trial framing. Safer for audiences who want to know the commitment length upfront.

- **Option C:** "Get My First Prioritized Board" -- Outcome-focused. Promises a deliverable, not access. Works well for audiences skeptical of software trials.

### Subheadline Alternatives

- **Option A:** "Connect your Asana or Trello board. Let TaskPilot sort your priorities by deadline, client value, and team capacity. Review in five minutes." -- More detailed, walks through the three inputs (deadline, client value, capacity) that matter to agency PMs.

- **Option B:** "Every morning, TaskPilot builds your priority list from the tasks already in your board. You review. Your team executes. No manual triage." -- Focuses on the daily workflow. Good for audiences who want to visualize the routine before trying it.
