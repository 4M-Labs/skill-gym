### Rewritten copy

**Headline (Primary):**
TaskPilot reads your project history and prioritizes tasks before you ask.

**Headline Alternatives:**

1. Stop guessing what comes next. TaskPilot handles prioritization.
2. Your projects move fast. TaskPilot keeps up.

**Subheadline:**
AI-powered task management for agencies that run on Asana or Trello. TaskPilot learns how your team works and surfaces the right tasks at the right time, so you spend less time in triage and more time shipping.

**Body Copy:**

Your team doesn't have a prioritization problem. They have a "which task do I work on next" problem. Every day, project managers spend 30 to 45 minutes deciding priorities, moving cards around, and asking "what should we focus on?"

TaskPilot watches how your team completes work, spots patterns in your project history, and recommends task priorities automatically. It connects to the tools you already use, pulls in your project data, and gives your team a clear order of operations without the manual sorting.

**What it does:**
- Connects to Asana or Trello in two clicks. No setup wizard, no config files.
- Learns your team's pace, bottlenecks, and patterns from past projects.
- Reorders tasks based on deadlines, dependencies, and real progress.
- Flags blockers before they stall your sprint.
- Runs a daily prioritization in the background so you open your board with a ready plan.

**Who it's for:**
Project managers at agencies who juggle multiple client projects, manage five or more people, and are tired of manually reprioritizing every morning.

**What it is not:**
TaskPilot does not replace Asana or Trello. It sits on top of your existing board and handles the prioritization logic you currently do by hand.

**CTA:**
Start your free trial. Connect your board in two minutes. See what a prioritized backlog looks like.

### Annotations explaining what was changed and why

| Original Element | What Changed | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Leverages Advanced AI to Optimize Your Task Workflow" | Rewritten to "TaskPilot reads your project history and prioritizes tasks before you ask" | The original used buzzwords ("leverages," "optimize," "workflow") that say nothing specific. The rewrite tells the reader exactly what the product does in plain language. |
| "Cutting-edge machine learning algorithms" | Removed entirely | No one outside ML research cares about the method. They care about the outcome. Replace "machine learning algorithms" with what the product actually does for the user. |
| "Utilize predictive analytics to streamline your productivity pipeline" | Rewritten to "watches how your team completes work, spots patterns, and recommends task priorities automatically" | "Streamline your productivity pipeline" is jargon soup. The rewrite uses concrete verbs and a specific outcome (task prioritization). |
| "Next-generation task orchestration" | Removed | "Next-generation" is a filler word that means nothing. "Orchestration" is a tech metaphor that confuses non-technical buyers. |
| "Revolutionizes how your team collaborates" | Removed | Every SaaS product claims to "revolutionize" something. This word has been diluted to zero. Replace with specific, testable claims. |
| No target audience mention | Added "Who it's for" section | The original copy talked about "your team" without specifying who on the team. Agencies are the target. State it directly. |
| No specificity about integration | Added "Connects to Asana or Trello in two clicks" | The audience uses Asana and Trello. They need to know the product works with their stack, not a generic "integrates with your tools" claim. |
| No objection handling | Added "What it is not" section | Buyers need to know if this replaces their current tool. Address that upfront to reduce friction. |
| No clear CTA | Added "Start your free trial. Connect your board in two minutes." | The original had no call to action. The rewrite gives a specific next step and removes ambiguity about what happens. |
| Exclamation points and hype tone | Replaced with calm, direct sentences | The instructions say no exclamation points. The audience (project managers) responds to clarity and confidence, not excitement. |
| Passive constructions like "Experience next-generation..." | Rewritten to active voice: "TaskPilot watches... spots... recommends..." | Active voice is easier to read and sounds more confident. |
| No metric or time reference | Added "30 to 45 minutes" as a concrete pain point | Specific numbers are more believable than vague claims. Even an estimated range grounds the problem. |
