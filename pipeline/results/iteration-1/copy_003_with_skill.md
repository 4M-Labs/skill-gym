## Annotations

### Why I made these choices

**Hero Section:**
- "One tool for every design your team creates" — leads with the outcome (one tool) rather than the product category (design platform). Speaks directly to the pain of using multiple tools.
- Named competitors (Canva, Figma) in the subhead to anchor positioning and capture comparison search intent.

**Pricing Tiers:**
- Added tier descriptors ("For small teams getting organized") so visitors can self-select without reading feature lists. Reduces cognitive load.
- "Most Popular" badge on Team plan uses social proof to guide the majority toward the target tier ($30/user/mo).
- CTA copy differentiates: "Start Free Trial" (lower commitment) vs. "Talk to Sales" (appropriate for enterprise). Added reassurance copy underneath each CTA to reduce anxiety.

**Comparison Table:**
- Directly names competitors to capture "Canva vs Figma vs Adobe" search traffic. Shows DesignFlow's differentiation without disparaging—the table speaks for itself.
- Highlighted "Built for teams" and "Approval workflows" as the key differentiators that justify switching.

**Social Proof:**
- Used specific metrics ("5 days to 2 days") and company sizes (280 employees, 150 employees) to match the target audience. Generic testimonials don't convert.
- Quoted outcomes (brand consistency, faster review cycles) rather than vague praise.

**FAQ:**
- Addressed the top 5 objections: switching costs, limits, pricing, trial terms, and storage. Each answer is short and direct.
- "No credit card required" appears three times across the page—once near every CTA.

**Final CTA:**
- Repeated the key reassurance: 14 days, full access, no credit card. Removes last-minute hesitation.

## Alternatives

### Headline Options

**Option 1 (Chosen): One tool for every design your team creates**
- Rationale: Focuses on the outcome (consolidation) and the audience (teams). Direct and benefit-driven.

**Option 2: The design platform your marketing team actually needs**
- Rationale: Uses "actually" to subtly acknowledge that current tools don't fit. Creates curiosity. Slightly more provocative—test with brand voice tolerance.

**Option 3: Design together. Stay on brand. Ship faster.**
- Rationale: Three short phrases that summarize the value prop. Works well for teams who already understand the problem and want speed. More action-oriented.

**Option 4: Stop designing in silos**
- Rationale: Problem-first headline. Works well for paid ads or landing pages where the audience already knows they have a collaboration issue. Less effective for organic traffic comparing tools.

## Key Copy Decisions

| Decision | Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Tier naming | Starter / Team / Enterprise | Industry standard. No surprises. "Team" signals collaboration by default. |
| CTA language | "Start Free Trial" | Action + benefit. "Free" reduces risk. "Trial" implies time-limited (not subscription pressure). |
| Price anchoring | Show monthly price | Marketing managers evaluate tools monthly. Annual option mentioned in FAQ for those ready to commit. |
| Comparison table | 6 features, 4 competitors | Enough to show differentiation without overwhelming. Focuses on collaboration, brand, and approvals—the key switching drivers. |
| Testimonial format | Quote + role + company size | Gives credibility. Company size (150-280 employees) matches the target ICP. |
