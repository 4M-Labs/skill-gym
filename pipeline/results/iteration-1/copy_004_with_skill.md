## Annotations

### Primary Headline Choices

- **"Your team shares passwords"** — Opens with what they already do. No judgment, no fear. Meets them where they are.
- **"Not spreadsheets. Not sticky notes."** — Names the exact alternatives they're using. Specificity creates recognition. If someone Googled "password spreadsheet for business," this line tells them we understand.
- **"One secure vault"** — Simple, concrete benefit. No jargon like "zero-knowledge encryption" or "AES-256." They don't care about the tech. They care about one place for everything.

### Subheadline Choices

- **"Stores every password, fills them instantly"** — Lead with the two actions they'll use daily. Utility first.
- **"Alerts you when a breach hits"** — Addresses the risk without fear-mongering. It's framed as a feature that protects them, not a threat that scares them.
- **"Built for businesses with 10 to 50 people, not enterprises with 10,000"** — Directly counters their objection. They think password managers are "enterprise tools." We tell them this one isn't. The number specificity (10 to 50 vs. 10,000) draws a sharp line.

### CTA Choices

- **"Start Free for 14 Days"** — Combines two low-friction signals: free + time-bound. The verb "Start" implies forward momentum, not commitment.
- **"Get SecureVault Free"** — Puts the product name in the CTA. Works when the headline doesn't name the product.
- **"Try It Free for 14 Days"** — "Try" is softer than "Start." Better for audiences still skeptical that they need this.

### Why No Fear-Mongering

The audience already knows shared spreadsheets are risky. They've probably had a close call — a file sent to the wrong person, a password changed without notice. They don't need to be told they're in danger. They need to be told there's a simple fix that doesn't require restructuring their whole workflow.

### Voice Notes

- No exclamation marks. Confidence doesn't shout.
- No "military-grade" or "bank-level." Overused and meaningless.
- No "seamless" or "effortless." They'll decide if it's effortless after they use it.
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs. One idea per section.
