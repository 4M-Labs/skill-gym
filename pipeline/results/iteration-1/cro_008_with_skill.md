# CRO Analysis: WriteRight Grammar Check Feature Page

**Page:** Grammar Check Feature
**Traffic Source:** Organic search ("AI grammar checker")
**Current State:** Headline "Grammar Check Feature", technical explanation of NLP/transformers, UI screenshot, "Try It Now" CTA

---

## Quick Wins (Implement Now)

1. **Rewrite headline to lead with benefit, not feature name.** "Grammar Check Feature" passes the 5-second test poorly -- a visitor scanning the page learns what it is called, not what it does for them. Change to a headline that mirrors the search intent: "Catch Every Grammar Mistake Before Your Reader Does" or "Write Error-Free Every Time -- AI Grammar Check." This aligns with the organic query "AI grammar checker" and immediately answers the visitor's unstated question: "Will this fix my writing?"

2. **Replace technical body copy with benefit-first language.** The current paragraph about NLP models, transformer architecture, and training data is developer-facing documentation, not conversion copy. Visitors searching "AI grammar checker" want to know: What mistakes does it catch? How fast is it? Will it sound like me? Rewrite to: "WriteRight scans your text in real time and catches grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors you'd miss -- without stripping your voice."

3. **Change CTA from "Try It Now" to value-communicating copy.** "Try It Now" is functional but generic. Replace with "Check My Writing for Free" or "Fix Grammar Mistakes Instantly." The CTA should echo the promised benefit and reduce perceived commitment.

4. **Add 3 before/after grammar correction examples directly on the page.** This is the single highest-impact addition for this page type. Users searching "AI grammar checker" are comparison-shopping. Show real corrections: "Their going to the store" corrected to "They're going to the store" with a brief explanation. This builds immediate credibility and demonstrates the product works.

5. **Move the UI screenshot below the benefit headline and examples, not above.** The screenshot currently likely sits without context. Placing it after the value proposition and examples means visitors arrive at the screenshot already understanding what they are looking at, increasing its persuasive power.

---

## High-Impact Changes (Prioritize)

1. **Add a trust signal block with quantified social proof.** The page has zero testimonials, review scores, or usage statistics. Add: "Trusted by 12,000+ writers" or a star rating from a review aggregator, or 2-3 short testimonials from users who improved their writing. Social proof is critical for free-tier SaaS tools where visitors are comparing multiple options.

2. **Restructure the page into a conversion flow: Headline (benefit) > 3 bullet benefits > Before/After examples > Social proof > CTA.** The current page appears to be: Title > Technical paragraph > Screenshot > CTA. This is a monologue, not a persuasion sequence. Reorder to guide the visitor from interest to desire to action.

3. **Address the #1 objection: "Is this better than Grammarly?"** Visitors searching "AI grammar checker" are almost certainly aware of Grammarly. Add a comparison section or a "Why writers choose WriteRight over Grammarly" block. Even if you cannot name competitors directly, a "How we're different" section handles the objection proactively.

4. **Add a "See it in action" interactive element or short demo GIF.** A static screenshot is passive. A 5-second animated GIF showing a correction in real time dramatically increases time-on-page and perceived product quality. This is especially effective for tool-based features.

5. **Add FAQ section addressing common objections.** Include: "Does it work offline?" "What languages are supported?" "Is my writing stored?" "Can I use it in Google Docs?" Each FAQ entry is an objection neutralized and an SEO opportunity for long-tail queries.

6. **Include a use-case strip: "Perfect for" section.** List 3-4 scenarios: emails, blog posts, academic papers, business proposals. This helps visitors self-identify and visualize using the product in their specific context.

## Copy Alternatives

### Current Headline: "Grammar Check Feature"

**Alternative 1:** "Catch Every Grammar Mistake Before Your Reader Does"
- Rationale: Creates urgency and frames the benefit in terms the reader cares about -- being perceived as polished. Matches search intent ("grammar checker") by confirming the tool does what they searched for.

**Alternative 2:** "Write with Confidence -- AI-Powered Grammar Checking"
- Rationale: Addresses the emotional benefit (confidence) rather than the mechanical function. "AI-Powered" matches the search query and signals modern technology. Works well for professional and academic writers.

**Alternative 3:** "Your Writing, Error-Free in Seconds"
- Rationale: Adds specificity (seconds) and implies ease. Short, scannable, and benefit-forward. Best for visitors who want fast results and are comparing multiple tools.

### Current CTA: "Try It Now"

**Alternative 1:** "Check My Writing for Free"
- Rationale: Mirrors the action the visitor wants to take. "My" makes it personal. "Free" eliminates the cost objection at the click moment.

**Alternative 2:** "Fix Grammar Mistakes Instantly"
- Rationale: Uses the visitor's language ("grammar mistakes") and adds a speed promise ("instantly"). Strong for visitors who are frustrated with errors.

**Alternative 3:** "Paste Your Text and See the Fix"
- Rationale: Describes the exact next step, reducing ambiguity. Works well if the tool has a paste-to-check flow. Low commitment framing.

### Current Body Copy: Technical explanation of NLP models and transformer architecture

**Alternative 1:** "WriteRight reads your text the way a sharp editor would -- catching grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors in real time without rewriting your voice. Paste in an email, essay, or proposal and get corrections in seconds."
- Rationale: Translates technical capability into user benefit. Includes specific use cases (email, essay, proposal) to help visitors self-identify. Avoids jargon entirely.

**Alternative 2:** "Powered by advanced AI trained on millions of real-world corrections, WriteRight identifies mistakes that spell-checkers miss -- from subject-verb agreement to misplaced commas. You keep your style. We keep you error-free."
- Rationale: Hints at technical credibility ("millions of real-world corrections") without alienating non-technical visitors. Addresses the common fear that AI will flatten your writing voice.

**Alternative 3:** "Most grammar tools catch typos. WriteRight catches the errors that make you look careless -- awkward phrasing, inconsistent tense, and unclear sentences. It's the difference between 'good enough' and 'polished.'"
- Rationale: Differentiates from basic spell-checkers. Frames the product as an upgrade, not just a tool. Creates a gap between the visitor's current state and the desired state.
