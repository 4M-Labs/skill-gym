# Annotations

## Voice and Tone Decisions

**Technical but warm, peer-level tone.** The copy reads like it was written by someone who has actually done the work. No buzzwords like "synergy" or "ecosystem play." Instead, concrete language that resonates with developers: "404 at 2 AM," "reverse-engineers undocumented endpoints," "sprint burn down."

**First-person plural ("we") throughout.** Creates a peer relationship rather than vendor-to-customer dynamic. The founders are talking to other engineers, not presenting a corporate front.

**Specificity over vagueness.** Instead of "thousands of customers," we say "200+." Instead of "significantly faster," we say "73% average integration time reduction." Numbers build credibility with technical audiences who distrust inflated claims.

**Pain-first, solution-second structure.** The hero opens with the problem, not the product. Technical decision-makers respond to empathy—they want to know you understand their specific frustration before you pitch a solution.

**No corporate speak.** Phrases like "synergy," "leverage," "best-in-class," and "seamless integration" are eliminated. The copy uses language developers actually use: "plumbing," "shipped code," "vendor black boxes."

**Features framed as benefits.** "API Contract Testing" is a feature. "Catch breaking changes before they hit production" is the benefit. Every capability section leads with what it means for the reader, not what it technically does.

**CTA is low-friction.** "Get Started Free" removes financial risk. "No credit card required" addresses the #1 objection. "30 minutes with an engineer, not a salesperson" speaks directly to the audience's dislike of sales processes.

**Values section reinforces trust.** Technical audiences are skeptical of marketing claims. The values section explicitly states commitments that address common developer objections: "no black boxes," "peer-to-peer," "if our product isn't the right fit, we'll tell you."

**Data table is scannable.** CTOs and senior devs scan before they read. The metrics table provides instant credibility without requiring a full page read.

**"What's Next" creates forward momentum.** Shows the company is actively investing, not stagnant. Also signals to potential customers that joining now means growing with the platform.
