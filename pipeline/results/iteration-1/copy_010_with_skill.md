# PetPal — Hero Section Copy

## Primary Headline

**Travel Without the Guilt. Your Pet Deserves Better Than a Kennel.**

## Subheadline

PetPal connects you with vetted, certified pet sitters who treat your pet like family. Every sitter passes a background check, holds a pet first aid certification, and meets your pet face-to-face before you book — so you can actually relax while you're away.

## CTA

**Find Your Sitter** — Free Meet & Greet Included

## Annotations — Why These Choices

**Headline: "Travel Without the Guilt."**
Leads with the emotional pain point — guilt. The target audience (25-40, traveling for work) doesn't have a logistics problem; they have an emotional one. The word "guilt" names the feeling directly, which builds instant rapport. The second sentence ("Your Pet Deserves Better Than a Kennel") reframes the decision as something they're doing *for* their pet, not just for themselves.

**Subheadline: Specificity builds trust.**
Instead of vague promises ("we care"), the subheadline names three concrete verification steps: background checks, pet first aid certification, video meet-and-greets. This uses the "Specificity Over Vagueness" principle — three verifiable facts beat one emotional claim.

**CTA: "Free Meet & Greet Included"**
Reduces friction. The word "free" removes risk. The phrase "included" signals this isn't an upsell — it's standard. The CTA action ("Find Your Sitter") is benefit-oriented, not company-oriented ("Sign Up" or "Join").

**Alternative 1: "Stop Worrying About Who's Watching Your Pet."**
Speaks directly to the anxiety of trusting a stranger. "Who's Watching" mirrors the exact language pet owners use when venting about kennels or unreliable sitters.

**Alternative 2: "Your Pet's New Favorite Person"**
Positions the sitter as someone your pet will love, not just tolerate. This counters the kennel fear of impersonal care. The subheadline uses "sends daily updates" — a specific feature that directly addresses the "out of sight, out of mind" anxiety working pet owners feel.

**Voice Calibration: Warm, Reassuring, Trustworthy**
No hard-sell language. No urgency tactics ("Limited spots!"). The copy earns trust by being specific and transparent about the verification process rather than making emotional claims without evidence.
