## Annotations

### Key Copy Choices

**Headline "Stop Building Reports. Start Delivering Them."**
- Leads with the pain (building) and contrasts with the desired state (delivering)
- Uses action verbs that mirror the audience's daily frustration
- Implies speed and efficiency without making a numerical claim in the headline

**"2-4 hours every week" in subheadline**
- Uses the exact number from audience research
- Specificity builds credibility and helps readers self-identify
- Time savings is the primary value prop, so it appears above the fold

**"No manual building. No copy-paste. No missed deadlines."**
- Three short fragments that hit different angles of the same pain
- Pattern interrupt from standard sentence structure
- Each fragment addresses a specific frustration from customer interviews

**"You Didn't Become a Marketing Director to Build Reports"**
- Validates the reader's identity and ambition
- Creates emotional resonance before presenting the solution
- Positions the feature as liberating, not just convenient

**"That's not strategy. That's admin work."**
- Short, punchy, opinionated
- Forces the reader to categorize their current behavior
- Sets up the solution as the "right" way to work

**CTA "Start Your Free Trial" over "Try It Free"**
- "Start" implies momentum and beginning something new
- "Your" makes it personal
- Clear, direct, no ambiguity about what happens next

**Pricing anchor with "Start Free" language**
- Reduces commitment anxiety
- "Scale When You're Ready" removes pressure
- Transparent pricing builds trust early in the funnel

**Supporting copy "No credit card required"**
- Removes the #1 friction point for SaaS trials
- Placed immediately after pricing to address objection in real-time

## Alternatives

### Alternative Headline Options

**Option 1: "Your Weekly Reports Shouldn't Take a Weekly Time Investment"**
- Rationale: Highlights the irony of current workflow. Creates cognitive dissonance that demands resolution. Works well for audiences who already know they waste time on reports.

**Option 2: "2-4 Hours a Week on Reports. Or Zero."**
- Rationale: Uses the specific time number as a hook. Binary choice framing makes the decision feel obvious. Best for retargeting audiences who've seen the problem framing already.

**Option 3: "What If Your Reports Wrote Themselves?"**
- Rationale: Asks a question that triggers imagination. Creates curiosity gap. Works best for cold traffic where the reader needs to envision the possibility before believing it.

### Selection Rationale

Chosen headline: "Stop Building Reports. Start Delivering Them."

- Most direct for the target audience (marketing directors at agencies)
- Uses action language that mirrors their daily experience
- Contrasts pain (building) with benefit (delivering) without being abstract
- Pairs well with the time savings subheadline for a complete argument on first view
