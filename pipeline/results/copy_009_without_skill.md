# Email Capture Section Copy — GrowthStack Blog

## Task
Write email capture section copy for GrowthStack blog (marketing analytics tool). Appears at bottom of every blog post about SEO, paid ads, and content marketing.

## Target Audience
Marketing managers at B2B SaaS companies.

## Voice
Professional, authoritative, value-first.

---

## Copy Options

### Option A — Direct Value Proposition

**Heading:** Stop Guessing. Start Measuring.

**Body:** Every week, we break down the analytics frameworks that top-performing B2B marketing teams use to turn data into decisions. No fluff. No vanity metrics. Just the signals that move pipeline.

**CTA:** Get the Playbook

---

### Option B — Authority-Led

**Heading:** What Separates Good Marketers from Great Ones

**Body:** Great marketers don't just run campaigns — they understand what's actually driving results. Each week, GrowthStack distills the latest in SEO, paid strategy, and content performance into one actionable brief.

**CTA:** Join 4,200+ Marketing Leaders

---

### Option C — Outcome-Focused

**Heading:** Your Competitors Are Already Doing This

**Body:** The B2B SaaS companies winning at organic and paid acquisition share one thing: they make decisions based on data, not instinct. Our weekly brief gives you the exact frameworks and benchmarks to close the gap.

**CTA:** Subscribe to the Growth Brief

---

### Option D — Concise & Direct

**Heading:** One Email. One Insight. Every Week.

**Body:** Actionable marketing analytics for B2B SaaS teams. SEO benchmarks, paid channel breakdowns, and content performance frameworks — delivered every Tuesday.

**CTA:** Subscribe Free

---

## Recommended Option

**Option A** — "Stop Guessing. Start Measuring." — delivers the strongest hook for the target audience. Marketing managers at B2B SaaS companies are overwhelmed by data but lack clarity. This framing positions the newsletter as the solution to their core pain point: translating metrics into meaningful action.

---

## Implementation Notes

- Keep CTA button text under 4 words for mobile readability
- Pair with a simple form: email field + subscribe button (no name field — reduces friction)
- Place below article content, above footer
- Consider adding social proof element (subscriber count or logo bar) for first-time visitors
