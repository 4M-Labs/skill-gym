## Framework Notes

**Observation → Problem → Proof → Ask**

1. **Observation**: Acknowledges CloudScale has PMF (compliment grounded in signal, not flattery)
2. **Problem**: Points to the gap — PLG motion likely doesn't exist or isn't structured
3. **Proof**: DevStream case study with specific numbers (28%, 3 months, 0 new hires)
4. **Ask**: Low-friction. No meeting request. Just "worth a quick look?" and a soft offer to share more.

**Why this works:**
- Opens with their world, not yours
- "Your growth story proves that" is specific enough to feel researched without being creepy
- The DevStream comparison is close enough (same industry, similar size) to feel like social proof, not a random case study
- The proof point addresses two objections at once: it works (28% lift) and it doesn't require headcount (no new sales reps)
- CTA is interest-based, not calendar-based

**Subject line rationale:**
- "cloudscale's trial flow" — lowercase, 3 words, looks internal. The reader will think a colleague or partner sent it. No pitch, no urgency, no name tricks.
