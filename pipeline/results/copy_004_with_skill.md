## Annotations

**Headline — "Stop sharing passwords through spreadsheets."**
Uses the "Never [unpleasant event] again" formula. Directly names the behavior the audience already knows is wrong. No fear-mongering — just states the facts. Addresses the real pain point (insecure sharing) without lecturing.

**Subheadline — "dead-simple way...without the complexity or cost of enterprise tools"**
Mirrors the audience's language. They already said enterprise tools are "overkill" — this copy validates that feeling and positions SecureVault as the middle ground. "Dead-simple" is casual and confident, matching the voice.

**CTA — "Start Free Trial"**
Action-oriented, specific, and low-risk. The word "free" reduces friction. Follows the skill's formula: [Action Verb] + [What They Get].

**Social Proof — "Trusted by 2,400+ small businesses"**
Specific number builds credibility. "Small businesses" signals this product is for them, not for Fortune 500 companies.

**Problem/Pain — "You know the spreadsheet has to go."**
Opens with a rhetorical question implied by the statement. Validates what they already think without being preachy. The second sentence ("Every time someone leaves...") shows the operational pain without catastrophizing.

**Benefits section**
One benefit per line. Each follows the pattern: [what it does] — [what that means for you]. No jargon. "One click" appears twice to emphasize simplicity.

**How It Works — 3 steps**
Reduces perceived complexity. Three steps signals "this is easy." No technical language.

**Pricing Anchor — "$4.99/user/month"**
Placed before the final CTA to anchor value. "No contracts. Cancel anytime." removes commitment anxiety.

**Final CTA — "Your team's passwords are one spreadsheet away from a breach."**
Restates the problem without fear-mongering — it's a factual observation, not a doomsday scenario. Follows with the solution path.
