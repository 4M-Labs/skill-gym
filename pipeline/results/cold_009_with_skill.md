**Skill used:** cold-email
**Framework:** Observation > Problem > Proof > Ask

---

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|

| **Product** | CallCoach - AI-powered call coaching tool for sales teams |

## Subject Line

coaching consistency across your team

*Why it works: 3 words, lowercase, internal-looking. Could be something a colleague sent about an ops problem. No pitch, no urgency, no first name.*

---

## Email

Karen,

At 200 people, you've got multiple managers running coaching their own way -- some reviewing every call, some winging it, some not looking at calls at all. The reps who happen to get the better coaches close more. Everyone else figures it out on their own.

RevenueStack was dealing with the same thing. 180-person sales enablement company, coaching all over the map. They brought in CallCoach to standardize what good looks like across their 35 SDRs. Win rates went up 19% in two quarters.

Worth exploring whether that maps to your team?

---

## Quality Check

- **Human-sounding?** Yes. Read aloud: conversational, no marketing-speak, no "I hope this email finds you well." Uses contractions, reads like a peer.
- **Would I reply?** Yes. The problem is real and specific to their situation. At 200 people, inconsistent coaching is almost guaranteed. The proof is concrete and from a company in the same industry.
- **Every sentence serves the reader?** Yes. Line 1 reflects their world (inconsistent coaching). Line 2 names the consequence (reps with better coaches win more). Line 3-4 tells a story about someone like them. Line 5 is a low-friction, interest-based ask.
- **Personalization connected to problem?** Yes. "200 people" is the exact trigger -- at that scale, coaching inconsistency is inevitable. "Sales enablement company" mirrors the proof company, making the parallel tight.
- **One clear ask?** Yes. "Worth exploring whether that maps to your team?" -- interest-based, easy to say yes with a one-line reply. No meeting request, no calendar link.
- **No forbidden patterns?** No "I hope this email finds you well," no "My name is X," no jargon, no feature dump, no HTML/images, no fake urgency.
