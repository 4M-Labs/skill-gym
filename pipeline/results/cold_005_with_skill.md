## Why This Works

- **Triggered:** Opens on their Vogue win, not your product. Shows you know their situation.
- **Peer voice:** Reads like a note from someone who understands DTC fashion, not a pitch.
- **Specific problem:** Creative testing as the bottleneck when scaling spend after a press moment -- a concrete, relatable challenge for a Marketing Director.
- **Proof > features:** One result (35% ROAS improvement, 10x more variants) beats listing platform features.
- **Low-friction CTA:** "Worth a quick look?" is easy to reply yes to. No calendar link, no 30-minute booking pressure.
- **No red flags:** No "I hope this email finds you well," no jargon, no HTML, no multiple links.
- **Subject line:** Looks internal, boring, not salesy. Gets opened.

## What I'd A/B Test

- **Subject line variant:** `vogue traffic`
- **Opening variant:** Lead with a question instead of the congratulatory note: "How many creative variants did your team ship last month?"
- **CTA variant:** "Open to comparing notes on how other DTC brands handle creative velocity after press hits?"
