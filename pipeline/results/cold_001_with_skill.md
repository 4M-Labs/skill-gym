### Skill Framework Applied

**Framework:** Trigger -> Insight -> Ask

| Element | Execution |
|---------|-----------|
| **Trigger** | Series C funding -- signals scaling, new channel complexity, budget expansion |
| **Insight** | Most B2B SaaS at this stage lack unified cross-channel ROI visibility |
| **Proof** | NimbusCRM case study: 60% reporting time reduction, 23% marketing-sourced revenue increase |
| **Ask** | 15-minute intro call, framed as low-commitment ("if they don't map, we'll both know") |

**Skill rules followed:**
- Subject line: 4 words, lowercase, no punctuation tricks, internal-sounding
- Opens with their world (funding event), not "I hope this finds you well"
- Lead with their situation -- "your team" dominates over "I/we"
- Personalization connects to the problem (funding -> channel complexity -> reporting chaos)
- One clear CTA (15-min call), interest-based framing ("Worth a quick call?")
- Proof over features -- one specific case study beats a feature list
- No jargon, no "synergy," no "best-in-class," no fake urgency
- Every sentence earns its place -- cut anything that doesn't move toward reply
- Voice reads as a peer who noticed something relevant, not a vendor following a template

**What would make this stronger:**
- A LinkedIn post from Sarah about marketing ops challenges
- Specific detail about Brightwave's tech stack (e.g., HubSpot, Salesforce)
- A reference to a mutual connection or shared community
- Brightwave-specific hiring signals (e.g., "saw you're hiring a Marketing Ops Manager")
